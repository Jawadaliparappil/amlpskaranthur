About AMLPS Karanthur

AMLPS Karanthur is a premier primary educational institution dedicated to providing holistic education to children. Established with the vision of nurturing young minds and shaping them into responsible citizens, our school has been a cornerstone of quality education in the Karanthur community.

We believe in a child-centered approach to education that emphasizes not only academic excellence but also character development, creative expression, and physical well-being. Our curriculum integrates modern teaching methodologies with traditional values to provide a well-rounded educational experience.

At AMLPS, we create a supportive and stimulating environment where children can explore, learn, and grow at their own pace. Our dedicated teachers guide students with patience and understanding, recognizing and nurturing their unique talents and abilities.

Karanthur AMLP was granted during the British era to address the educational needs of the Muslim community. The first student was admitted on April 3, 1929. The first student was Kuttyali, the son of Moideen Koya, Chettukuyil, Karanthur. The school was started by Pavukandathil Ahmed Haji, a prominent family in Karanthur, who bought land from the government for Rs. 50 in an auction. After his death, the school management was handed over to his son P.K. Seethi Haji . He continued as the school manager from 29.5.1980 to 5.3.1986. On 6 March 1986, P.K. Seethihaji sold the school to the Muslim Education Trust, an organization based in Karanthur. After that, K. Tharuvikutty Haji, who was the president of the organization until 1996, was the manager. Then, M. Tharuvikutty Haji, who was the president, and then M. Biran Haji, the current president, held the position of manager. The buildings were renovated and some changes were made under the leadership of the trust and it is now functioning in good condition.
